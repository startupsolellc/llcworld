How to Start an LLC in Alabama: Step-by-Step Guide

Nazmi Ozer February 2, 2025

Forming a Limited Liability Company (LLC) in Alabama is a strategic move for entrepreneurs looking for liability protection and business flexibility. This step-by-step guide walks you through the essential requirements, costs, and timelines to form an LLC in Alabama.

How Much Does It Cost to Start an LLC in Alabama?

The cost of forming an LLC in Alabama depends on several factors, but here are the essential expenses:

  • Filing Fee: The Alabama LLC Certificate of Formation costs $200 when filed online and $208 when submitted by mail.

  • Business Name Reservation: Before filing, Alabama requires you to reserve your LLC name, which costs $25.

  • Registered Agent Fee: If you hire a professional Registered Agent, expect to pay between $50–$300 per year.

  • Operating Agreement (Optional but Recommended): If you hire a legal professional to draft this, costs may range from $100 to $500.

  • EIN (Employer Identification Number): Free when obtained from the IRS.

  • Business Privilege Tax: The minimum tax is $100 per year, with additional fees based on revenue.

Total costs for forming an LLC in Alabama typically range between $200 and $500, depending on optional services.

How Long Does It Take to Get an LLC in Alabama?

The processing time for forming an Alabama LLC varies based on how you file:

  • Online Filing: 1-2 business days (Recommended for faster approval).

  • Mail Filing: Typically takes 1-2 weeks.

  • Name Reservation: Instantly online or 1-2 weeks via mail.

  • EIN Application: Instantly when applied online at the IRS website.

To speed up the process, file everything online and ensure all required information is correctly submitted.

Steps to Forming an LLC in Alabama

1. Search Your Limited Liability Company (LLC) Name

Before filing, you must ensure that your LLC name is available in Alabama. Here’s how:

  • Conduct a name search using the Alabama Secretary of State’s Business Entity Search.

  • Alabama requires you to reserve your LLC name before filing. You can do this online ($25 fee) or by mail.

  • Your LLC name must include “Limited Liability Company,” “LLC,” or “L.L.C.”

2. Choose a Registered Agent

An Alabama Registered Agent is required for every LLC. The Registered Agent can be:

  • A person who resides in Alabama.

  • A business authorized to operate in Alabama.

The agent receives official legal documents on behalf of your LLC. Many business owners choose professional Registered Agent services to maintain privacy and compliance.

3. File an LLC Certificate of Formation

To officially form an Alabama LLC, you must file a Certificate of Formation with the Alabama Secretary of State.

  • Filing Fee: $200 (online), $208 (mail).

  • Filing Methods: Online at the Alabama Secretary of State’s website or by mail.

  • Required Information: LLC name, Registered Agent details, business purpose, and organizer information.

  • Processing Time: 1-2 business days (online), 1-2 weeks (mail).

4. Create an Alabama LLC Operating Agreement

An Operating Agreement is not required in Alabama but highly recommended. This document outlines:

  • Ownership structure of the LLC.

  • Management and voting rights.

  • Profit and loss distribution.

  • Procedures for adding or removing members.

Having an Operating Agreement protects your LLC by defining business rules and reducing potential disputes among members.

5. Get an EIN for Your LLC

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) is essential for tax purposes and opening a business bank account.

  • How to Apply: Free through the IRS website.

  • Processing Time: Instant when applied online.

  • Why You Need It: Required for hiring employees, opening a bank account, and filing taxes.

6. File Your Initial Business Privilege Tax Return

Alabama requires new LLCs to file an Initial Business Privilege Tax Return (Form BPT-IN) within 2.5 months of formation.

  • Minimum Tax: $100 per year.

  • Due Date: Within 2.5 months of LLC formation.

  • Filing Method: Online via the Alabama Department of Revenue.

This tax is required annually, so ensure compliance to avoid penalties.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  1. What is the fastest way to form an LLC in Alabama?

    • Filing online through the Alabama Secretary of State’s website is the fastest method.

  2. Is an Operating Agreement required in Alabama?

    • No, but it is highly recommended for LLCs with multiple members.

  3. Can I be my own Registered Agent in Alabama?

    • Yes, as long as you have a physical address in Alabama.

  4. What taxes do Alabama LLCs pay?

    • LLCs must pay the Business Privilege Tax, with a minimum of $100 annually.

  5. Can a non-resident form an LLC in Alabama?

    • Yes, non-residents can form and own an Alabama LLC.

  6. Do I need a business license to operate an LLC in Alabama?

    • It depends on the type of business; some require specific licenses.

  7. What is the difference between a domestic and foreign LLC?

    • A domestic LLC is formed in Alabama, while a foreign LLC is registered in another state but operates in Alabama.

  8. How do I dissolve an LLC in Alabama?

    • You must file a Certificate of Dissolution with the Secretary of State.

  9. How can I check if my LLC name is available?

    • Use the Business Entity Search tool on the Alabama Secretary of State’s website.

  10. Can I change my LLC name after formation?

    • Yes, by filing an amendment with the state.

  11. How long does an EIN application take?

    • When applied online, it is issued instantly.

  12. Do Alabama LLCs need an annual report?

    • No, but they must file an annual Business Privilege Tax return.

  13. Can an LLC have just one owner?

    • Yes, a Single-Member LLC is allowed in Alabama.

  14. Can I use a PO Box as my business address?

    • No, Alabama requires a physical street address.

  15. Do I need to renew my LLC every year?

    • No, but you must file the Business Privilege Tax annually.

  16. What is a Series LLC?

    • It is a type of LLC that allows separate divisions under one entity.

  17. What happens if I don’t pay the Business Privilege Tax?

    • Your LLC may face penalties or dissolution.

  18. Can I add members to my LLC later?

    • Yes, by amending your Operating Agreement and informing the IRS.

  19. Is an Alabama LLC a good choice for small businesses?

    • Yes, due to its liability protection and tax flexibility.

  20. Can an LLC own another LLC in Alabama?

    • Yes, this is called a parent-subsidiary LLC structure.

By following these steps and FAQs, you can confidently form and maintain your Alabama LLC.

 

Final Thoughts

Starting an LLC in Alabama involves multiple steps, from name registration to tax filing. By following this guide, you can efficiently form your LLC while ensuring compliance with Alabama state laws.
