How to Apply for an EIN as a Non-US Resident

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) is essential for any LLC operating in the United States. Without an EIN, a business cannot open a U.S. bank account, apply for Stripe or PayPal business accounts, or conduct legal business activities in the country. This guide will walk you through the EIN application process step-by-step, ensuring that you can obtain your EIN quickly and efficiently.

What is an EIN?

EIN stands for Employer Identification Number, sometimes referred to as a Federal Tax ID. It is a 9-digit number assigned by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to identify a business for tax and reporting purposes. An EIN follows this format: XX-XXXXXXX.

Why Do You Need an EIN?

¬†

An EIN is required for various business activities, including:

  • Opening a U.S. business bank account (Mercury, Payoneer, Wise, etc.).

  • Registering with payment processors like Stripe or PayPal.

  • Filing U.S. tax returns if necessary.

  • Hiring employees in the U.S.

  • Complying with IRS regulations for foreign-owned LLCs.

Without an EIN, your LLC will not be able to legally operate in the U.S. financial system.

Can Non-US Residents Apply for an EIN?

Yes! Whether you live inside or outside the U.S., if you own a U.S.-based LLC, you must obtain an EIN. The good news is that you do not need to be physically present in the U.S. to apply for an EIN. The process is completed remotely by submitting IRS Form SS-4.

Step 1: Download Form SS-4

Before filling out the application, download the latest version of IRS Form SS-4 from the official IRS website. Though the form has not changed since 2019, always verify you are using the most recent version.

Step 3: Obtain a Virtual Fax Number

The IRS only accepts EIN applications via fax for non-US residents. Since most people do not have access to a traditional fax machine, you can use an online fax service such as Fax Plus.

üìå Suggested Virtual Fax Service: Fax Plus (starting at $5.99/month)

You need to keep your fax service active until you receive your EIN, as IRS may send communications through fax if they require further verification.

Step 4: Submit Form SS-4 to the IRS

Once you have completed your SS-4 form, send it to the IRS via fax.

üìå IRS Fax Number for EIN Applications: +1 855-641-6935

‚úÖ Submission Checklist:

  • Ensure SS-4 Form is correctly completed and signed.

  • Attach your LLC formation documents (Articles of Organization/Certificate of Formation).

  • Double-check the fax number and confirm that the transmission was successful.

Step 5: Wait for Your EIN Approval

The IRS processes EIN applications within 4 to 6 weeks, but it may take longer depending on IRS workload. Your EIN confirmation letter (CP 575) will be mailed to the U.S. mailing address provided on Form SS-4. If you use a Registered Agent service, they will forward the document to you digitally.

It is recommended to check your business address mailbox regularly for IRS notifications. If you provided a fax number, IRS might fax your EIN directly, written at the top of the SS-4 form.

Applying for an EIN Without an SSN or ITIN

Many non-U.S. residents worry about obtaining an EIN without an SSN or ITIN. Fortunately, an SSN or ITIN is not required to get an EIN for a foreign-owned LLC. Simply leave the SSN/ITIN field blank on Form SS-4.

What If You Don‚Äôt Receive Your EIN?

If your EIN does not arrive within six weeks, you can re-fax your SS-4 application to the IRS. Write ‚ÄúSECOND REQUEST‚Äù at the top of the form and submit it again to the fax number listed above.

Additionally, you can contact the IRS EIN department at +1 267-941-1099 (not toll-free) to inquire about your application status.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How long does it take to get an EIN for a foreign-owned LLC?

Typically, it takes 4 to 6 weeks, but delays may occur depending on IRS workload.

2. Can I apply for an EIN without a U.S. address?

Yes, but you must provide a valid U.S. mailing address for EIN confirmation delivery.

3. Do I need an SSN or ITIN to get an EIN?

No, non-US residents do not need an SSN or ITIN to apply for an EIN.

4. What happens if my EIN application is rejected?

You may need to correct errors and reapply or contact the IRS for clarification.

5. Can I apply for an EIN online as a non-US resident?

No, the IRS requires non-US residents to apply via fax or mail.

6. Can I use a virtual address for my EIN application?

Yes, but it must be a real, physical business address in the U.S.

7. What is the cost of obtaining an EIN?

The EIN itself is free, but fax services may charge a small fee.

8. Will the IRS notify me if there is a problem with my application?

Yes, they may fax or mail you if additional information is needed.

9. Can I reapply if I don‚Äôt receive my EIN?

Yes, after six weeks, you can re-fax your application with ‚ÄúSECOND REQUEST.‚Äù

10. Can I get an EIN if I don‚Äôt have a company yet?

No, an EIN is only issued to legally registered entities.

Final Thoughts

Obtaining an EIN as a non-US resident is a straightforward process when following the correct steps. By correctly filling out Form SS-4 and submitting it via fax, you can ensure smooth approval.
