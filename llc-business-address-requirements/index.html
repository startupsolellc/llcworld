Securing a U.S. Business Address: Legal Requirements and Best Practices

Nazmi Ozer February 9, 2025 LLC Formation for Non-Residents

Understanding the Importance of a Business Address When Forming an LLC in the U.S.

One of the key aspects of setting up a company in the United States is securing a proper business address. Whether forming an LLC or a Corporation, state laws require every company to have an official address for legal and administrative purposes. However, the type of address, its intended use, and regulatory requirements vary depending on the business structure and operations.

Why is a Business Address Necessary When Forming a U.S. Company?

Legal Notices and Official Documents

Each state mandates that companies have a valid business address to receive legal correspondence. This address is where critical documents, such as court notices, IRS communications, and state filings, are sent. It cannot be a PO Box; it must be a physical address where someone is available to receive official documents.

Required for Banking and Financial Transactions

To open a business bank account as a non-U.S. resident, your company must have a verifiable address. Financial institutions will send debit cards, account statements, and other important documents to this address. Providing an incorrect or shared address may result in undelivered banking materials and potential account restrictions.

Needed for Marketplace Seller Accounts

E-commerce platforms like Amazon, Etsy, and Walmart require a unique business address when setting up a seller account. Shared or virtual addresses are generally not accepted, and even if initially approved, they can lead to account suspension during the verification process. To avoid this issue, businesses need a suite number (unique business address) from a reliable mail forwarding service.

Legal and Commercial Requirements

The legal and commercial aspects of obtaining a business address vary by state. However, some fundamental requirements apply across the U.S.:

State Registration

All U.S. companies must register a business address in their formation state. This can be the business's physical location or a registered address for official correspondence. A PO Box is not accepted; a real office address is preferred.

Registered Agent Requirement

Every LLC or Corporation in the U.S. must designate a Registered Agent an entity or individual residing in the state of formation responsible for receiving legal documents. This is a mandatory requirement in all states.

Required for Tax Filings

The IRS and state tax agencies require businesses to list a valid address for tax correspondence. Tax notices, audits, and legal notices will be delivered to this location. Using a random or non-verifiable address can cause compliance issues.

Business Licenses and Permits

Some states and local governments require a physical address to issue business licenses. While this may not apply to online businesses, entrepreneurs should consider this factor if they plan to establish a physical presence in the future.

Registered Agent Address

The Registered Agent address is used solely for legal and regulatory correspondence. It is a requirement for LLC formation, ensuring that legal documents can be received and forwarded to the business owner. Most Registered Agent services provide online portals where scanned documents can be accessed.

Unique Business Address (Suite Number)

A suite number is crucial for businesses selling on marketplaces like Amazon, Etsy, and Walmart, as these platforms do not accept shared Registered Agent addresses. To avoid account suspensions, businesses should opt for premium mail forwarding services that provide unique suite numbers.

While standard Registered Agent services offer shared addresses, they also provide premium mail forwarding at an additional cost. However, failing to pay monthly fees can result in the cancellation of the suite number.

The Wyoming Advantage

Unlike other states where premium mail forwarding services require a monthly fee (typically $20/month), Wyoming allows businesses to obtain a unique suite address for a one-time payment of $15. This address is permanently valid and suitable for marketplace registrations and business use.
